Decoding H.R. 1: Key Medicaid Changes and Their Targets

An interactive breakdown of the major policy shifts affecting Medicaid beneficiaries and program structures.

Impacts on Beneficiaries

Community Engagement / Work Requirement

Primary Affected Group:

👥All adults covered by the ACA Medicaid expansion (including childless adults and parents of children over 13).

Finding: Mostly correct, but the scope is broader.

Per Section 71119 of the bill, this applies to all adults made eligible for Medicaid through the ACA expansion (`Sec. 1902(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII)`). This includes parents of children over 13, not just childless adults.

New Cost-Sharing

Primary Affected Group:

💰All ACA Medicaid expansion adults with incomes above 100% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL).

Finding: Mostly correct, but the scope is broader.

Per Section 71120, this applies to all ACA expansion beneficiaries (`specified individual`) with family incomes over 100% of the FPL. This includes parents, not only childless adults.

6-Month Redeterminations

Primary Affected Group:

📅All adults covered by the ACA Medicaid expansion.

Finding: Mostly correct, but the scope is broader.

Per Section 71107, this requirement applies to the entire ACA expansion population, mirroring the scope of the work requirement.

Stricter Alien Eligibility

Primary Affected Group:

🌍All lawfully present immigrants not in specified exempt categories (including adults, children, and parents).

Finding: Correct.

Per Section 71109, the restriction applies to both Medicaid and CHIP, thus affecting lawfully present immigrants of all ages who do not fall into one of the specified exempt categories (like lawful permanent residents).

Impacts on Programs & Funding

Waiver Budget Neutrality

Primary Affected Group:

📋All groups who rely on `Section 1115` demonstration waivers for innovative services or eligibility (e.g., the homeless, some individuals needing long-term care, those with complex health conditions).

Finding: The impact is much broader.

Per Section 71118, this requirement applies to all `Section 1115` demonstration waivers, not just those for long-term care. While those programs could be affected, other innovative waivers (e.g., for the homeless, justice-involved individuals) would also face severe restrictions.

Key Takeaways & Narrative Advice

Focus on the "Expansion Population"

The first three major changes—work requirements, new cost-sharing, and 6-month redeterminations—are precisely targeted at adults covered by the ACA Medicaid expansion. Framing the story around this group as the central target of the reforms will create a strong narrative focus.

Highlight the Compounding Effect

These policies are not isolated. For example, the "6-Month Redeterminations" amplify the risk and administrative burden of the "Work Requirement," creating a more potent combined effect. This "policy stacking" is a key part of the story.

Frame "Budget Neutrality" as the End of Innovation

The requirement for waiver budget neutrality effectively restricts the ability of local governments like D.C. to design innovative service models tailored to local needs. This can be narrated as a shift where federal control tightens, local flexibility is lost, and the most vulnerable ultimately pay the price.
